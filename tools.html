What Are Prepress & Packaging Tools?

Prepress and packaging tools help designers prepare files for professional print production. These tools bridge the gap between digital artwork and physical manufacturing, ensuring accurate color reproduction, barcode scannability, packaging structure, and compliance with industry standards.

Tools like TAC calculators prevent ink overload on the printing press, dieline generators create perfect structural box layouts, and barcode safe zone checkers ensure quiet zones are large enough for retail scanners to read.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is TAC in printing?

Total Area Coverage (TAC) refers to the total percentage of CMYK ink used in a specific print area. If the TAC limit (usually 280% - 300%) is exceeded, the ink will not dry fast enough, causing smudging and transfer to other sheets during production.

Why is a barcode safe zone important?

A barcode safe zone (or quiet zone) is the blank space on the left and right of a barcode. It ensures optical scanners and lasers can identify where the barcode begins and ends without interference from surrounding graphics.

What is a dieline?

A dieline is a flat vector template that shows the exact cut lines, fold lines, and glue flaps required to manufacture a 3D physical package or box.
