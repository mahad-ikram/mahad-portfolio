What is a Nutrition Label?

A nutrition label provides essential information about the nutritional value of a food product, including calories, fats, carbohydrates, protein, and vitamins. It is legally required for most packaged foods globally to ensure consumer transparency.

FDA vs EU Nutrition Label Formats

FDA Nutrition Label (US 2016+ Standard)

The FDA mandates a highly specific typographic hierarchy. "Calories" must be printed in a massively oversized, bold font. The label relies on a strict order of nutrients and calculates a % Daily Value (%DV) based on a 2,000-calorie diet, requiring exact 8-point and 1-point separator hairlines.

EU Nutrition Declaration

The European Union uses a cleaner, table-based approach. Values are standardly required to be shown "Per 100g" or "Per 100ml" to allow consumers to easily compare different products. It uses "Energy (kJ/kcal)" and groups specific saturated fats and sugars directly underneath their parent macros.
